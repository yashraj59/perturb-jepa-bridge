## Diagnostic Follow-up: Objective, Split, Projection, and Readout Audits

### Hypothesis
The 100-run failure was not enough to localize the defect. The follow-up hypothesis was that collapse could be caused by either objective terms, split/evaluation geometry, projection normalization, dropout-masked train traces, or weak RNA condition readout.

### Implementation
Added and ran synthetic-only CPU diagnostics:

- `scripts/diagnose_synthetic_lite_objectives.py`
- `scripts/diagnose_synthetic_split_geometry.py`
- `scripts/diagnose_encoder_projection_mode.py`

Added focused readout repair options:

- encoder pooling modes: RNA `mean_tokens`, image `mean_patches`
- bridge RNA condition readout modes: `encoder`, `pseudobulk`, `encoder_plus_pseudobulk`

### Results
Objective ablations did not finally collapse on traced train batches, but eval-mode collection collapsed even on train condition bags. True latent oracle retrieval was perfect and raw ridge RNA-to-image retrieval beat random, so the synthetic target is learnable. Train-mode no-gradient variance was much higher than eval-mode variance, but biological R2 became strongly negative, indicating dropout-masked false health.

Mean pooling plus no-dropout variance/covariance regularization cleared the hard collapse flag but retrieval stayed random and RNA latent R2 stayed negative. Token/patch pooling did not pass. Pseudobulk readout made RNA latent R2 positive but collapsed RNA variance and still retrieved at random.

### Decision
No promotion. Continue only with focused diagnostics/repairs. The next useful mechanism is pseudobulk-specific projection geometry that preserves RNA biological signal without collapsing variance.

The bridge failure is not lack of biological signal. It is unstable learned shared-space geometry. Classical low-rank cross-covariance methods should identify a safe condition-level RNA/image shared space before further JEPA training.

### Hypothesis

Added `counterfactual_rna_residual` to the bridge config. When enabled, `counterfactual_rna = source_rna_pseudobulk + counterfactual_rna_delta`. The counterfactual script zero-initializes the RNA delta decoder in residual mode so the initial prediction is exactly source-as-target.

- `scripts/train_clone_counterfactual_decoder.py`

In the three-seed check, protected retrieval/R2/batch metrics had zero regression, and all seeds improved direction, logFC, pseudobulk, and top-DE overlap. ## Post-210 Synthesis: Strict GPU Continuation

**Scope**: Experiments 111-210 completed 100 new documented synthetic CUDA trials, all focused on seed-2 counterfactual program recovery with protected PLS geometry frozen.

**Result**: No full counterfactual gate pass. There were no protected-geometry regressions and no real-data or external-resource use.

**Main learning**: MLP program-decoder loss rebalancing cannot repair seed 2. Linear program decoders help, and train-split-only prefit ridge initialization is a strong non-promoted near miss: seed-2 program recovery rises to about `+0.25`, but top-50 DE overlap remains below gate. The next synthetic-only repair should target top-DE overlap using train-pair target-delta magnitudes or synthetic generator metadata, not real marker/pathway resources.

## Experiment 211: seed2_sparse_residual_rank4_topde50

**Hypothesis**: The seed-2 blocker may come from perturbation-specific sparse deltas being swamped by the invariant PLS/clone representation. A separate perturbation-only residual channel should recover program deltas while preserving the protected shared geometry.

**Family**: L, literature-informed sparse perturbation residual head.

**Implementation**: Added `perturb_jepa.models.sparse_perturbation_residual.SparsePerturbationResidualHead` and `scripts/train_sparse_perturbation_residual_head.py`. The frozen PLS bridge is wrapped but not modified; `z_inv` is the frozen RNA shared clone feature, while `z_pert` is produced from source program means plus direct no-batch biological metadata (`perturbation_id`, `cell_line_id`, `dose`, `time`). The head predicts only `delta_hat`, with `x_cf = x_control + delta_hat`, using a program-factorized term plus a learned low-rank gene dictionary.

**Literature rationale**: PerturbedVAE motivates separating sparse perturbation-specific signal from invariant expression (https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19343). PertAdapt motivates upweighting perturbation-sensitive genes (https://sciety.org/articles/activity/10.1101/2025.11.21.689655). Systema motivates comparing against perturbed/matching-mean baselines (https://brbiclab.epfl.ch/projects/systema). Conditional Monge Gap remains the transport fallback if residual deltas fail (https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08328). TxPert-style biological graph priors are deferred to real-data/OOD work and were not used (https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-026-03113-4).

**Initialization / identity preservation**: The program decoder final layer and low-rank dictionary are zero-initialized, so the initial counterfactual is exactly source-as-target. The protected PLS readout and distillation clone are frozen; max readout drift after training was `0.00000000`.

**Tier result**: Tier 1 `synth_micro` seed 2, 100 steps, device `cuda`. Gate pass `False`, protected geometry `True`.

**Diagnostics**: Program recovery improved from `0.0000` to `0.2877`, and direction accuracy improved to `0.5793`. However, logFC correlation was `-0.0830`, pseudobulk changed by `-0.0012`, and top50 overlap stayed flat at `0.4058`. Mean delta/target ratio was only `0.0149`, program contribution ratio `0.9244`, low-rank contribution ratio `0.1066`, effect mask fraction `0.5199`, decorrelation loss `0.357783`, and outside sparsity loss `0.013436`.

**Matching-mean baseline**: The no-batch matching-delta mean baseline had exact key coverage `1.0000`, program recovery `0.3502`, direction accuracy `0.5312`, logFC correlation `0.1268`, and top50 overlap `0.4150`.
