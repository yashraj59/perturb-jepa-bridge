## Interpretation
The residual selector family has generated useful evidence but no promotable model. Held-out oracle capacity exists, so the residual target is not mathematically impossible. The blocking issue is that train-only residual selection remains optimistic for the actual held-out perturbation split, even after row-wise abstention, residual cones, and leave-perturbation-out train calibration.

The next family should not be another cap, cone, projection, or row-wise selector on the same contract. The next smallest evidence-based move is a data-contract/benchmark redesign diagnostic that makes train-internal calibration predictive of the scored held-out task, or proves that current synthetic held-out residual headroom is below reliable train-only selection.
